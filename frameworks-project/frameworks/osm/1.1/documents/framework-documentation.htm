xpath - /record/classify/classification
Framework version - 1.1

Definition - an indication as to whether the resource is refereed or non-refereed
Obligation - required
Minimum occurrences - 1
Maximum occurrences - 1
Data types - string
Domain - controlled vocabulary
Domain example - Refereed

Controlled vocabulary information

  • Number of levels - 1
  • Level 1 is Classification - an indication as to whether the resource is refereed or non-refereed

Term and definitions

  • Refereed - an indication that a given resource was subjected to a process of critical evaluation by one or more experts on the subject, responsible for determining if the subject of the article falls within the scope of the publication and for evaluating originality, quality of research, clarity of presentation, etc.

Collaboration

xpath - /record/classify/collaboration
Framework version - 1.1

Definition - the type or extent of the relationships between the entities responsible for the intellectual content of the resource
Obligation - optional
Minimum occurrences - 0
Maximum occurrences - 1
Data types - string
Domain - controlled vocabulary
Domain example - UCAR only

Controlled vocabulary information

  • Number of levels - 1
  • Level 1 is Collaboration - the type or extent of the relationships between the entities responsible for the intellectual content of the resource

Term and definitions

  • No UCAR Authors - at the time of its creation, no contributors to the intellectual content of a given resource were UCAR employees
  • UCAR only - all contributors to the intellectual content of a given resource were, at the time of creation, UCAR employees
  • UCAR and University - the intellectual content of a given resource was contributed by UCAR employees and representatives from a university
  • UCAR and Other - the intellectual content of a given resource was contributed by UCAR employees and representatives from another, non-university institution.
  • UCAR, University and Other - the intellectual content of a given resource was contributed by UCAR employees, representatives from a university, and representatives form another, non-university institution

Cataloging best practices

Things to do

  • This is an optional field.
  • This field cannot be repeated.
  • While this field is optional, complete it if collaboration information is known.

Things to avoid

  • Do not complete this field if is does not apply or collaboration information is sketchy or unknown.

Examples

  • UCAR, University and Other: COMET modules use UCAR, university and other govenment lab personnel to write the content.

Date - type

xpath - /record/coverage/date/@type
Framework version - 1.1

Definition - describes a given date's relevance to the resource
Obligation - required metadata if parent tag is present
Minimum occurrences - 0
Maximum occurrences - 1
Data types - string
Domain - controlled vocabulary
Domain example - Published

Controlled vocabulary information

  • Number of levels - 1
  • Level 1 is Date type - describes a given date's relevance to the resource

Term and definitions

  • Accepted - date on which a work was selected for publication
  • Created - date on which a resource was produced
  • Digitized - date on which an analog or physical resource was transferred into or replicated by an electronic format
  • Fiscal - date pertaining to the budgetary calendar of a given institution
  • In press - date on which a work was in the process of being published
  • Modified - date on which a resource was altered from its previous state
  • Published - date on which a given resource formally appeared in print
  • Reviewer assigned - date on which an individual was assigned to formally appraise a resource
  • Submitted - date on which a work was sent to a publisher for consideration

Cataloging best practices

Things to do

  • This is a required field.
  • This field can only be repeated if an entirely new date entry is made.
  • Use the term Created for works that exist but are not published.
  • Ues the term Fiscal to represent UCAR's fiscal year which begins October 1 and ends September 30th every year.
  • Use to this field especially for resources within the publishing lifecycle.

Things to avoid

  • Do not use this field to represent a embargo time periods. Use the field of dateRange instead.

Examples

  • Created: the group of SOARS papers were created and digitized but they were not published in a journal.

Definition - the type and/or source of a resource identifier
Obligation - required metadata if parent tag is present
Minimum occurrences - 1
Maximum occurrences - 1
Data types - string
Domain - controlled vocabulary
Domain example - REPORT

Controlled vocabulary information

  • Number of levels - 1
  • Level 1 is ID number type - the type and/or source of a resource identifier

Term and definitions

  • arXiv - identifier for entries in the arXiv.org repository of preprints for mathematics, physics, computer science, quantitative biology and statistics
  • CALL - a unique identifier used to identify a specific copy and relative location of an item within a library’s collection
  • Citation/Article - a unique reference label to ensure the persistent and actionable identification of resources
  • DOI - Digital Object Identifier: a set of unique code used by publishers and other disseminators of digital content to ensure the persistent and actionable identification and interoperable exchange of that content

  • ISSN - International Standard Serial Number: a unique, standardized eight-digit standard number assigned to a specific serial title.
  • PUBID - identification number for entries in the UCAR publications database
  • REPORT - identifier used to reference a published report; particularly those published by government entities and other agencies
  • SRef-ID - a unique URL used by the SRef database to ensure the persistent and actionable identification of the resources it references
  • WMO - report identification numbers assigned by the World Meteorological Organization
  • WOS - identifier used to reference works indexed by the Web of Science

Title - type

xpath - /record/general/title/@type
Framework version - 1.1

Definition - the genre of a given work’s intellectual content
Obligation - required
Minimum occurrences - 1
Maximum occurrences - 1
Data types - string
Domain - controlled vocabulary
Domain example - Article

Controlled vocabulary information

  • Number of levels - 1
  • Level 1 is Type - title - the genre of a given work’s intellectual content

Term and definitions

  • Abstract - a brief, objective representation of the essential content of a book, article, speech, report, dissertation, patent, standard, or other work, presenting the main points in the same order as the original but having no independent literary value. [Online Dictionary for Library and Information Science (OLDIS)]
  • Animation/moving image - a medium of expression consisting of a series of related images, which create the illusion of movement when viewed in rapid succession
  • Article - a self-contained prose composition on a fairly narrow topic or subject, written by one or more authors and published or submitted for publication under a separate title in a collection or periodical containing other works of the same form
  • Artifact/physical object - an object made or modified by the work of one or more persons (replicas excluded) or a natural object
  • Bibliography/index - a systematic list or enumeration of written works by a specific author or on a given subject, or that share one or more common characteristics (language, form, period, place of publication, etc.)
  • Blog/forum/email list - Blog: a web page that provides frequent continuing publication of web links and/or comments on a specific topic or subject (broad or narrow in scope), often in the form of short entries arranged in reverse chronological order, the most recently added piece of information appearing first [Online Dictionary for Library and Information Science (OLDIS)]
  • Blog/forum/email list - Forum/email list: an email discussion forum that allows individuals to subscribe and automatically receive messages posted to the list by other subscribers. Participants may also post their own messages and replies for distribution to the other subscribers to the list [Online Dictionary for Library and Information Science (OLDIS)]
  • Book chapter - one of two or more major divisions of a book or other work, each complete in itself but related in theme or structure to the division preceding and/or following it
  • Book review - an evaluative account of a book, usually written by a qualified person for publication in a newspaper, magazine, or journal
  • Book/monograph - Book: a written work or composition that has been published and bound as a single volume
  • Book/monograph - Monograph: A relatively short book or treatise on a single subject, that is complete in one volume, detailed and scholarly but not extensive in scope, and usually written by a specialist in the field
  • Collection - a group of related items that have something in common like a shared topic, intended audience, genre or institution that has sponsored their creation, or shared provenance
  • Comment - a substantive article, letter, or editorial that challenges, refutes, supports or expands upon a previously published work [Online Dictionary for Library and Information Science (OLDIS)]
  • Correspondence - letters, memoranda, notes, postcards, email, and other written and addressed messages exchanged between two or more people, usually archived with the personal papers of the correspondents [Online Dictionary for Library and Information Science (OLDIS)]
  • Encyclopedia - a book or numbered set of books containing authoritative summary information about a variety of topics in the form of short essays, usually arranged alphabetically or classified in some manner
  • Image - a single visual representation
  • Interactive resource - materials designed to respond to input from a human being, usually in the form of commands, data and/or actions
  • Journal/magazine/periodical - Journal: a periodical devoted to disseminating original research and commentary on current developments in a specific discipline, subdiscipline, or field of study, usually published in quarterly, bimonthly, or monthly issues, and sold by subscription
  • Journal/magazine/periodical - Magazine: a popular interest periodical usually containing articles on a variety of topics, written by various authors in a nonscholarly style. Most magazines are heavily illustrated, and contain advertising [Online Dictionary for Library and Information Science (OLDIS)]
  • Journal/magazine/periodical - Periodical: a serial publication with its own distinctive title, containing a mix of articles, editorial, reviews, columns, short stories, poems, or other short works written by more than one contributor, generally published at regular stated intervals of less than a year, without prior decision as to when the final issue will appear
  • Manual - a book used as a reference in the operation of a task, computer program, procedure, job or other function
  • Manuscript - a written and, traditionally, unpublished work or composition
  • Newsletter - a serial publication consisting of no more than a few pages, devoted to news, announcements, and current information that is of interest primarily to a specialized group of subscribers or members of an association or organization who receive it as part of their membership or affiliation
  • Notes - brief explanations or descriptions that usually supplement the understanding of a work, subject, or object
  • Poster - a visual medium created to publicize a forthcoming event, or communicate scholarly or scientific work, with or without illustration
  • Presentation/webcast - a broadcast, podcast, or formal representation of ideas communicated in person or via the internet
  • Proceedings - the published record of a conference, congress, symposium, or other meeting sponsored by a society or association
  • Report - a record of research findings, research still in progress, or other technical findings, usually bearing a report number and sometimes a grant number assigned by the funding agency
  • Software - a computer program, application, or model that must be run on a computer server
  • Sound - recorded auditory material that can be heard by the human ear
